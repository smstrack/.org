Becomes only the 3rd head track coach in school history

Wannamaker takes the reigns as head coach

J.J. Wannamaker begins his tenure at SM South as the head track and field coach in his 11th season of coaching track and field. Coach Wannamaker will oversee the organization and coordination of the girls and boys track and field teams as well as serving as the Varsity Sprints coach.

Coach Wannamaker comes to SM South after serving as the Girls Sprint Coach at SM North in 2007. In his one season at North, Coach Wannamaker saw 5 of his individuals and two relay teams qualify for the 6A State Meet. 1 individual and 1 relay team earned All-State honors in 2007.

From 2002 until 2007, J.J. served as the Head Track and Field coach at St. Marys High School in St. Marys, KS. St. Marys competed at the 3A and 2A levels during his tenure at St. Marys. In his time at SMHS, J.J. coached 8 athletes to individual school records and guided 2 4x100 relay teams to school records. Coach Wannamaker guided 34 individuals and 3 4x100 relay teams to state meet qualifications. All three relay teams and 16 of the individuals earned All-State honors. Under Coach Wannamaker’s guidance MaryJo Mott, class of 2007, won 5 state championships in the sprint events. The 2007 Lady Bears also placed 3rd in the team standings at the state meet, earning the first state track and field trophy in the history of St. Marys High School.

In 2000 and 2001, J.J. served as the Sprints and Pole Vault coach at St. Thomas Aquinas High School in Overland Park. Coach Wannamaker guided 8 individuals and 6 relays teams to state meet births. All-State honors were earned by 4 of the individuals and 2 of the relay teams.

During the 1999 season, Coach Wannamaker was on staff with SM North as the Pole Vault coach guiding one athlete to the state meet.

During the summers from 1999 through 2003, Coach Wannamaker worked with USATF and AAU athletes on various club teams. 22 of J.J.’s athletes qualified for the AAU or USATF National Junior Olympic championships with 6 of the athletes earning All-American honors.

J.J. is a 1998 graduate of Ottawa University with a degree in Biology. At Ottawa, J.J. was a 4 year member of the Cross Country and Track and Field programs, serving as the Cross Country and Distance team Captain his Junior and Senior years. Coach Wannamaker graduated from Rossville High School in Rossville, KS in 1994. At the high school level, Coach Wannamaker was a 3 time All-State athlete in Cross Country and Track and Field.

Coach Wannamaker is married to Lindsey and has two children, Chloe and James. Chloe and James are at most of the track and field meets and love to watch “Daddy’s” kids compete.
